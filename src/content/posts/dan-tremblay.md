## How/when did you get your start as a musician and what instruments do you play?

My mom started teaching me piano when I was 6 or 7 years old, and I've been playing ever since. I played a bit of tenor sax in high school and have dabbled in guitar, though have only started playing guitar more regularly very recently.

## How did you discover ambient music and who are your influences?

I think my first real foray into ambient music was hearing "Sweet Pea" by Lowercase Noises. Though I suppose when I listen to that song now, it's perhaps more fleshed out and almost has a post-rock vibe to it. But I think that was the moment that I began really listening to Andy Othling's stuff.

## What is your approach to making ambient (or other) music?

My approach is really the same for any music that I make, which is the lowest common denominator. It's so easy with digital music making now to throw dozens and dozens of tracks on a given song, but I always find it's more trouble than it's worth. Your song inevitably ends up sounding smaller because so much of the frequency spectrum has been taken up. So I'm always aiming to use as few tracks as possible to get your point or emotion across.

## What are you working on now? Have you collaborated with anyone recently?

Most of the recent collaborations have been in the role of producer/mixing engineer. I'm currently working on an EP for voiceunmuted. I will also be producing tracks for Eddie Brown, Lauren K and Jazzvke, hopefully all this year (2023).

Lisa Cuthbert and I are currently working on a combined song which I am super excited about. I plan on using Mouad Violon on a new version of "Empty Swing" which I hope to release early next year.

Luna Keller and Weathering Grains will appear on "You Are Loved" which is a track I wrote specifically for people suffering with mental illness.

I recently came across a YouTuber from Finland by the name of Timo Vehkaoja whom is an absolutely brilliant photographer turned filmmaker. I suggested to him that I create a custom track for one of his projects and he seemed very keen, so hopefully that happens. I would love to have an ongoing relationship with a filmmaker.

## Who would you like to work with in the future?

I would love to do a song with Max Willow. And this is a long shot, but I would love to work with Kieth Kenneff and SYML. Never say never.

I also love the work of another filmmaker, Roman Hense whom I found on Youtube. I would love to have my music featured in one of his videos.

## What piece of gear has been the most inspirational for you recently and why?

I absolutely love the VSX headphones from Slate Audio. It's so much easier to dial in the low end with those beasts.

## What other hobbies do you have (outside of music)?

I am pretty obsessed with videography, though my skills are not nearly as polished as my music production. I recently purchased a new camera and lens and can't wait to get out and shoot with it. I plan on filming new footage and pairing it with older footage of "Empty Swing" when I re-release it.

## Is there anything else you want to share that we didn't ask about?

I absolutely love the musical community on Twitch. The platform is FAR from perfect, but the people I have met have literally changed my life in so many positive ways.
