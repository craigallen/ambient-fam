**Artist Names:** Scott Bennett, And out of Weeks

## How/when did you get your start as a musician and what instruments do you play?

I loved to sing as a child. I guess I started as soon as I could talk. Instruments came a little later. I started playing guitar at around 10 years old. I pick at other instruments from time to time like harmonica, mandolin, bass. But guitar and vocals are my main instruments.

## How did you discover ambient music and who are your influences?

I discovered ambient music through its connection and similarities to post-rock. I am a big fan of bands like Mogwai and This Will Destroy You. When my friends and I started a post-rock band of our own, I took to the internet to find how to make some of those sounds and discovered Andy Othling.

## What is your approach to making ambient (or other) music?

My approach to making ambient music is pretty simple. I do the generative thing. I play some simple loops into my early chain loop pedals and kind of let them go through my board. I'll record that for 10-20 minutes to make sure I can grab as much variance as I can in a timely manner.

Then I might repeat the process after twisting some knobs or using various combinations of modulation. Then it's just splitting, cutting, pasting, and crossfading all the parts and sounds that I like the best and fit well together musically within the DAW.

## What are you working on now? Have you collaborated with anyone recently?

I just released my first full length album as And out of Weeks in October. I've also been tracking some guitar parts and backing vocals for an upcoming Dt Surgeon release. I haven't been collabing much lately, but I think I might have to rekindle a little group chat that includes some active members of the community.

## What piece of gear has been the most inspirational for you recently and why?

The piece of gear that has been the most inspirational to me is the Chase Bliss Habit. It especially made its impression on me in the past year while recording my album.

It just makes generative looping better by adding so much variance on top of a three minute loop and I think that's just amazing. It lends nicely to the generative process, giving those little magical moments more chances to appear during a recording take. If I could, I would have a second one in parallel with my existing one.

## What other hobbies do you have (outside of music)?

Outside of music I have a deep love for Magic The Gathering. I play the Legacy and EDH (commander) formats. I also love boardgames, horror movies, video games, and petting cats. I recently started taking Brazilian jiu-jitsu classes as well.
