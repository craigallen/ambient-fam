**Artist Name:** The Missing Note

## How/when did you get your start as a musician and what instruments do you play?

I may have been forced into a piano school when I was 8, and I had no interest at it during that time. So, it was short lived. Fortunately, that period gave me the necessary basic until today. Then in 1995, guitar became trendy among friends in high school so I picked one up and learn it on my own with books and cassettes. I ended up playing for a school band. Years passed, life came and music took a backseat after that.

Being a full time musician isn't exactly in the books; in fact it isn't that long ago, which was 3 years ago. It wasn't entirely planned and we'll probably need a book to fit the whole story. But I have been a cancer patient since 2009 and treatments eventually left me 'speechless' (literally) and I lost my job/career in 2019. I started picking up the guitar again for mental distractions. Noodlings, became a song. A few songs became a collection and I didn't know what to do with them. My wife suggested to just release it somewhere? I googled, and the rest is history.

## How did you discover ambient music and who are your influences?

I think like many in the ambient community, Andy (Lowercase Noise) has been the main influence and inspiration. Andy's music literally pulled me out from a dark chapter of myself (mostly due to the cancer) and it's like I could almost relate my life's story around his music. His 2014 album "This is for our sins" is still actively played on my list. I also listen to a lot of Olafur Arnalds, which has more influenced my playstyle and compositions more than anything.

## What is your approach to making ambient (or other) music?

A picture speaks a thousand words, but I'll say music tells an entire story. The best story I know, would be my own. I'd imagined my life as a movie and try to create a soundtrack for whichever chapters of my life I felt like sharing as a story. Usually sad and tragic ones creates the best moments of emotional attachments to the song. But there are happier ones that made it in some of my work.

## What are you working on now?

Apart from my weekly ambient streams on Twitch, I'm starting a new project/album based on the concept of luminal spaces. The concept itself is new to me, but I think it'll break away from my usual styles and give a fresh perspective composing. I'm also in the process of creating a catalogue mainly for sync licensing. I have a few tracks going into sync now, so I hope that will open new opportunities for me in 2024.

## Have you collaborated with anyone recently? Or, who would you like to work with in the future?

After 2 years doing this on my own, I came to realize collaborations are something I really enjoy working on. Whether I write the parts or let my collaborator have their own influence, I find myself being able to put a different energy into my work. My recently released album "Symphonic Reverie", released Dec 1st, is a testament to that.

## What piece of gear has been the most inspirational for you recently and why?

Basically, my guitar and midi keyboard. I'm currently using the Nektar LX88+ and a telecaster by Stagg. It may be the only instruments I will ever play on, but if there is an opportunity, I'd take up a cello.

## What other hobbies do you have (outside of music)?

Apart from music, I run a pretty full schedule as a stay-at-home dad. The kids schedule are packed and will easily run half my day. But I have recently picked up gaming on my PC again, which I find helps with giving my mind an escape and a break from reality.
